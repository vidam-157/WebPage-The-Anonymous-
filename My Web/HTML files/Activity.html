Top Most Affected Attacks by Anonymous

(1) Operation Chanology

Date: January 21, 2008
Victim: Church of Scientology.


This is the first known Anonymous attack which introduced the group to the world. A copyright violation claimed by the Church of Scientology against posting a video to YouTube has prompted a full-on assault by Anonymous. This a video feartured by Tom Cruise proclaims, in part, that Scientologists are the only experts on the mind, was banned by YouTube as a request of the Church of Scientology.


Anonymous attacked the church considering this as an "Internet censorship" which they consider as an Internet mischief. They launched a distributed denial-of-service attacks (DDoS), and soon after, black faxes, prank calls, and other measures intended to disrupt the Church's operations.


After the focus of the protest shifted to legal methods, as nonviolent protests and an attempt to get the Internal Revenue Service to investigate the Church of Scientology's tax-exempt status in the United States. Later the Church of Scientology started referring to Anonymous as "cyberterrorists" perpetrating "religious hate crimes" against the church.

(2) Operation SONY

Date: April 2, 2011
Victim: Sony Computer Entertainment


The anonymous attacked the Sony PlayStation network in 2011 against the SONY company's unfair lawsuit against mainly George Hotz and two other called Héctor Martín Cantero and Sven Peter, for eight claims including violation of the DMCA, computer fraud, and copyright infringement due to their activity hack and providing some jailbreak information to the internet.


The attack is known as the PlayStation Network outage (also the PSN Hack). This made approximately 77 million Sony accounts of PS3 users compromise and the personally identifiable data of all account users were been exposed. These data also contained the credit card numbers, PIN codes and bank details of the account users. The company's PlayStation network paused for nearly 3 weeks globally making management loss of $168 billion which was one of the largest data security breaches in history. It surpassed the TJX hack which affected 45 million customers in 2007.

(3) Bank Of America Attack

Date: March 14, 2011
Victim: Bank Of America


In an effort to expose Bank of America's alleged corrupt and unfair mortgage practices, Anonymous leaked a trove of internal e-mails reportedly sourced from a seven-year employee of the bank on bankofamericasucks.com. The self-styled Anonymous Intelligence Agency additionally leaked 320MB of emails and other information that suggests the bank is running an online intelligence gathering operation against hacktivists. This includes memos from IT contractor TEKsystems to the bank's security staff, reporting chat room and social network reconnaissance. These reveals on the bank made its stock market to plummet rapidly and go bankrupt.


(4) Justice for MegaUpload

Date: January 19, 2012
Victims: FBI, Universal Music, Recording Industry Association of America(RIAA), Motion Picture Association of America(MPAA)


Megaupload is a website allowed users to upload and share large files including films and TV shows but was shut down by the US Department of Justice with the complain of allowing copyright infringement.


In their point deciding this is unfair, the anonymous set up a distributed denial of service (DDoS) type attack to the FBI, Universal Music, RIAA and MPAA as their servers cannot be handle it. The attacks were perpetrated by a collective of more than 5,000 internet users and apparently using more than 27,000 computers. Anonymous did not stop there, leaked personal details of the executives in the above companies and made at least a 500 USD loss for a single company and made justice for Megaupload.


While it was the Megaupload shut-down that prompted Anonymous to act, the attacks also seemed to be in protest against organizations including the RIAA and MPAA who supported the Stop Online Piracy Act bills, currently being discussed in US Congress.

(5) Operation Payback

Date: December 8, 2010
Victims: Visa, MasterCard,PayPal,MPAA,IFPI


The US government informed that the WikiLeaks website is releasing top secret diplomatic cables to the public. The anonymous hackers were also joined with WikiLeaks with the process. But when the US government made the news public, PayPal, Mastercard and Visa companies stopped dealing with the website by freezing account and shutting down servers. So that the company lost the ways of receiving their funds.


The anonymous standing with the WikiLeaks planned an attacked for this. “Aiplex” was Software to launch DDoS attacks on websites that did not respond to takedown notices. The original plan was to attack Aiplex Software directly, but after decided to launch the attack against the websites of copyright stringent organizations, they launched the attack for Motion Picture Association of America (MPAA) and International Federation of the Phonographic Industry (IFPI) which was under the ministry of sounds. The attack caused a combined downtime of 30 hours to both websites.


Then they turned around to the side of payment sites which stopped donations to the "Wau Holland Foundation", which involved in raising funds for WikiLeaks. Anonymous declared war on 8th December 2010 against the Visa, Mastercard and PayPal and carried out a DDoS attack to the companies.


The Ministry of Sound estimated the cost of the attack on their sites as being £9,000, while the IFPI's costs were more than £20,000 and the BPI's more than £4,000. The financial impact on MasterCard and Visa were not revealed to the court. However, it was estimated at least to be a million-pound loss.

Isis, we will hunt you, take down your sites, accounts, emails and expose you. From now on no safe place for you online. You will be treated like a virus and we are the cure. We own the internet."



After these cases ISIS already has a challenge to keep their social media account in twitter as also the twitter itself has taken actions to remove accounts that identifies as promoting terrorism.

(7) Operation Titstorm

Date: February 10, 2010
Victim: Austrailan Goverment


The Australian government was ready to bring out an internet filtering legislation. By this the government made censorship in pornography of small-breasted women (who are thought to be under age) and female ejaculation. Anonymous launched a distributed denial-of-service type attack to protest the country’s evolution toward internet censorship using this situation. The anonymous group's ultimate goal is to secure the internet freedom of the public.


Anonymous shut down the Australian Parliament House website and almost took down the Department of Communications website. Government officers has been spammed pornographic emails, faxes and prank phone calls. The attack series was named as "Operation Titstorm".



(8) Fine Gael Website Attack

Date: February 25, 2011
Victim: Fine Gael Irish political party


Looking at the list of victims hit by the hacker group, the Fine Gael is a very small attack. During the 2011 Irish General Elections, Anonymous took over the group's new website and posted the above image with the text, "Nothing is safe, you put your faith in this political party and they take no measures to protect you. They offer you free speech yet they censor your voice. WAKE UP!" Furthermore, the page’s title changed to read: ‘The problem with politicians is they lie’ – a play on the party’s own message, that politicians talk too much and listen too little. Later the party confirmed that the details of 2,000 people who had the connection with the website had been compromised by the attack.



(9) Operation Tunisia

Date: February 25, 2011
Victim: Fine Gael Irish political party


The Tunisian Revolution started against the country’s embattled leadership was fought over a number of things as government corruption, unemployment, poor living conditions. But a major sticking point was the lack of freedom of speech. Under this situation the Anonymous hackers announced “Anonymous is willing to help the Tunisian people in this fight against oppression, Cyber-attacks will persist until the Tunisian government respects all Tunisian citizens’ right to free speech and information and ceases the censoring of the internet”.


The anonymous enraged with this behavior of the Tunisian government's unilaterally administration and attacked 8 of the government websites which are the president, prime minister, ministry of industry, ministry of foreign affairs, and the stock exchange websites. The attack was conducted as the sound of the speechless as promised.
